Paint Warfare

Role: Secondary Lead Developer, Community Manager

Dates: July 2022 - March 2024

Platform: Unity Game Engine (v.2022)

"A fast-paced online arena FPS with jetpacks, hang gliders, fast respawns, 6-minute rounds and instant matches"

As secondary lead developer, I worked very closely with Alex (the lead developer) to create sophisticated core systems to greatly enhance key aspects of the game. Working with an unofficial contract-based system, the first major piece of code I created was a modular keybind system, the first of its kind to be incorporated around the Unity default Input system.

After aiding & cooperating on systems such as a sophisticated anit-cheat, reworking aspects of weapon physics & incorporating new cosmetics, I was assigned with creating a set of powerups for the game, including a sticky healing-kit (following the release of team-based modes), a unique radar ability, & more.

Throughout this coding work, I was also a major member of the Paint warfare socials. I made sure that raids, spam & innapropriate content created on community group chats or comments was removed & punishments were handled accordingly, and I assisted with the creation of content advertising the game.


This was an incredibly fun project which greatly expanded my skills not only with Unity multiplayer, github / version control & C#, but also managing a community & being able to be fluid with the work I complete.

Tire Change

Role: Lead (& only) Developer

Dates: Mid 2022 - Early 2023

Platform: Unity Game Engine (v.2022)

A super fast-paced offline endless-platformer game with one-finger controls, where the player has to escape the police by drifting on hovering platforms.

NOTE: this was the latest footage i could find, I had greatly improved the AI & incorporated sound effects after this, along with some visual improvements & much better looking levels

Featuring low poly, colourful visuals, it was a great project to get a grasp at making a full game in unity. Fully solo, I had to make UI, develop every core script, balance colours to create a unique stylised look, experiment with particles & ai for the first time, and learn how to get it all working on mobile.

Unfortunately, it isn't on any app store as my computer bricked with all of the old files on it :(. Nevertheless, it was a fun side project to learn the basics of mobile game development, monetisation, design & more.
